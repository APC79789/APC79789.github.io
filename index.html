Beginning My Crochet Journey 🧶

I'm not sugarcoat it. It. was. HARD. It's a craft that can be both simple and overwhelmingly frustrating at first. Here's my personal experience and guide to walk you through those very first steps and, most importantly, to reassure you that the STRUGGLES are a normal part of the process. It also makes you appreciate Crochet Artists more but that's my perspective so....

Think of this as your patient friend, guiding you stitch by stitch. Let's begin!

Gathering The Stuff

You don't really need much to start. Keeping it simple is the key to not feeling overwhelmed.

  • One Crochet Hook: Start with a size H-8 (5mm) or I-9 (5.5mm). It's a comfortable middle-ground size for beginners.
  • One Ball of Yarn: Choose a smooth, light-colored, worsted-weight yarn (it will say "4" on the label). A light color helps you see your stitches clearly. Avoid dark, fuzzy, or textured yarn for now.
  • A Pair of Scissors: Any small pair will do.
  • A Yarn Needle: This is a blunt needle used for weaving in your ends at the end of a project.
  • Another Tip: If you want to crochet a Doll of choice , Use a 6mm or 7mm hook with thick wool yarn . I started crocheting with motivation to create dolls of my own characters!

The Very First Crochet Patterns to Learn

Every crochet project begins with two foundational skills: the slip knot and the foundation chain.

1. The Slip Knot

This is how you attach the yarn to your hook. It creates a secure, adjustable loop that becomes your first stitch. There are many ways to make it, but find one that feels comfortable for you.

2. The Foundation Chain

This is simply a series of loops, or "chains," that form the base of your work. You'll "yarn over" gonna s (wrap the yarn over your hook) and pull through a loop to create each chain. For now, just practice making a long, even chain. Don't worry about what it's for yet—just get the motion down.

Embracing the Struggles ✨

This is the most important section. You will struggle. And that is 100% okay. Every person who has ever picked up a hook has been right where you are. Here are the most common hurdles:

  • "My tension is all wrong!" Some stitches will be ridiculously tight, and others will be loose and floppy. This is pretty normal! Your hands are learning a new muscle memory. It will even out with time.
  • "I can't tell where to put my hook!" Looking at a chain or a row of stitches can feel like trying to read a foreign language. It takes practice to recognize the top two loops of a stitch. Be patient.
  • "My project is getting wider/narrower!" Accidentally adding or skipping stitches is a classic beginner move. Happens to everyone 😌. Counting your stitches at the end of each row is a great habit to build.
  • "My hands hurt!" You're holding your hook and yarn in a new way. Give yourself breaks. Don't grip the hook like you're trying to kill it. Relax!

Quick Tips for Beginners

  • Watch slow-motion videos. Seeing the motion from different angles really helps.
  • Don't be like me who rushed to try and make a complicated crochet, Trust me.
  • Start with a small project, like a simple coaster or a dishcloth.
  • Don't be afraid to unravel! Crocheting is just one long knot. You can always pull it out and try again.
  • Find a community! Online forums or local craft stores are great places to ask questions.
  • There is also free patterns to try out for beginners if that helps!
  • In the end, It will be feel VERY achieving and satisfying.
